How to find like minded people?
While ~60% of global population consider themselves either lonely or not having meaningful connections we don't have effective tools to discover like-minded people around.
1 Shallow matching factors Existing matching algorithms are based on either pictures or too common interests (such as music, food, etc.), oriented on retention and may not always bring the most potential candidates together
2 Lack of privacy We don't have many places on the Internet where we can be sincere in our opinions without fear of our personal data or identity being compromised or bullied
3 It takes too much time Even once matched people don't know much about each other. It's hard to start a conversation and takes much time to discover core values and habits
Web3.0 protocol for digital personality Allkinds is a web3 protocol and dapp that enables users to discover individuals who share their values, insights, and opinions with a high degree of precision.
1 Values Allkinds matches people based on what they consider important, requiring creative input from humans.
2 Full privacy Decentralization and cryptography ensure that your data and privacy are protected, allowing you to be sincere without fear of being compromised.
3 Know each other Once connected people can see how they are similar and/or different thus learning a lot about each other.
How it works When meeting new people, we ask questions to get to know each other. By comparing our own opinions on these questions to the other person's answers, we can determine whether or not we want to continue the conversation. On Allkinds it happens the same:
1 Questions People anonymously ask yes/no questions about what they consider important, answer it by themselves indicating the importance of every question and answer to questions from people around.
2 Cohesion and search We calculate the cohesion score between two users as a percentage of the same answers weighted upon importances from both users. One can search for the most resonating person, the least, or anything in between.
3 Magic Once agreed to connect two users can compare their answers to the same questions (without revealing answers in the UX/UI) thus learning a lot about each other in minutes. Meditate on this.
Meta profile of values While asking and answering questions users are building their meta profiles. They basically create and mark up data. Taking into account that importance for every question can be virtually any number every profile is going to be unique.
1 Digital personality Basically meta profile represents a dynamic user-generated digital personality that can be considered a soul bound token. Together with a reputation factor meta profiles can extend digital identities to a level of individual's values, traits, interests.
2 AI-powered Our AI-engine semantically understands questions, categorises them, and creates additional connections within the data, enabling prediction scores to be generated between two profiles even in the absence of common questions.
3 Social graph Allkinds can be seen as a private social graph where people connected upon what they consider important. Besides these potential connections users can connect to each other and create their private social graphs of meaningful connections.
Why protocol
Compatibility between people in terms of personal values plays significant role in many spheres of our lives. Allkinds is an open protocol and can be integrated with any web2 or web3 services. It does:
  • provide an interface for users to build and manage their meta profiles, search for other people, manage the social graph, built-in messenger and more
  • provide an interface for users to participate in DAO
  • provide a full set of public functions that would allow builders to interact with Allkinds
